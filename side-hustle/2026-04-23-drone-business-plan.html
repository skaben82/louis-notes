Drone business plan — 12-month beginner to pro

23 April 2026 Side Hustle

Months 1–2: Learn, Register, Get Legal

  • Buy drone (DJI Mini 4 Pro or DJI Air 3 / Mavic 3 Pro)
  • Get CAA Operator ID (£10/year) and Flyer ID (free)
  • Book and pass A2 CofC (~£60–£100)
  • Get commercial insurance (EC785/2004 compliant — e.g. Coverdrone, Moonrock)
  • Start a practice logbook

Months 3–4: Skill Building & First Portfolio

  • Practice flights: smooth movements, figure-8, reveals, orbits
  • Practice in different lighting — sunrise, golden hour, twilight
  • Learn editing: DaVinci Resolve (free) or Adobe Premiere Pro
  • Build mini-portfolio (6–10 clips), edit 1-minute showreel

Months 5–6: Stock Footage Uploads

  • Sign up to Shutterstock, Adobe Stock, Pond5, Artgrid
  • Aim for 30–50 clips uploaded in 4K minimum
  • Use keyword-rich titles and descriptions

Months 7–8: First Client Work

  • Approach local businesses: estate agents, hotels, golf courses, tourism boards
  • Offer discounted intro package
  • Post snippets weekly on Instagram and TikTok

Months 9–10: Scaling & Specialising

  • Review which clips sell best, film more of those
  • Pick a niche: real estate, events, tourism, construction
  • Invest in ND filters, extra batteries

Month 11: Brand & Marketing Push

  • Register as sole trader with HMRC
  • Build simple website with showreel, services, contact form
  • Gather testimonials from early clients

Month 12: First £1,000 Month

  • Run local promo — £99 aerial shoot for new clients
  • Bundle drone footage with short social media edits
  • Keep uploading 10–20 stock clips a month
  • Explore retainer deals with estate agents, builders, tourism companies

End of Year Targets

  • Stock library: 150+ clips online
  • Client base: 5–10 paying clients
  • Monthly income: £500–£1,000+ (mix of stock and direct jobs)
