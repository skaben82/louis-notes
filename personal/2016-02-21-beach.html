Beach

21 February 2016 Personal

The coastline stretched from left to right,so from wide that bob had to turn his head to view it in its entirety. Picture perfect, split halfway, blue water separated green hills from Blue sky. On the hill, Vineyards spread out in perfectly formed lines almost as if combed by some huge hand. Patches of green and darker green trees were on the vista. Dark brown rock faces dropped to reveal a snaking grey Road. The gentle rumble of 1950s cars could be held as they passed. These were broken up by the occasional raucous exhaust noises of motorbikes. Two up, A passenger clung to the back of the rider clad in black, only the breeze of the motion preventing them from melting in their leather costumes. As bob scanned the water, 2 birds flew by in a parallel pattern mirroring some red arrow formation flight. A lone swimmer made triangle shapes with his arms, propelling himself first straight towards the bar and then perpendicular at a slightly slower pace towards a floating platform. The platform had a silver steel ladder against one of its edges allowing the tired swimmer a bed to rest and take in the bright sunshine. The aromas of cigarette smoke from the nearby French girls tickled bob's nostrils. He looked up to see the source of the odour and was met by the sunglass covered eyes of a slim girl with a pink low cut top. She spoke quickly in French to her engrossed friend, the pair conversing in a rhythmic oscillating volume. A blonde hair man stood in front of Bob."have they got WiFi here, mate? "he asked. His eyes scanned bobs phone which was on the bar table. " I don't think so" he replied "you can check at the bar" the blonde man apologised for the interruption and went back to his table, shook his head at his young blonde son and equally blonde wife. The sound of the waves caught bobs ear, the tide was drawing away from the shoreline. A selection of rocks of various colours began to reveal themselves. As each wave came in and out the sound got a slight bit louder. The seagulls screeched as they realised they were caught in the middle of a undulating wave. Flapping their wings they rose above the crest of the waves and flew a few centimetres above the water rising to land on the roof of the tower at the end of the promenade.
On the floating platform the number of resting swimmers had grown to seven. Bodies were strewn across the wooden surface in a criss cross pattern like some sort of human jigsaw puzzle.
