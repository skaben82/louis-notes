Before you do anything else, I would redo the vacuum leak testing with a smoke tester. You can plug it in to a port at the rear of the valve cover and make sure there is not some vacuum leak anywhere including the valve cover. Usually the valve cover will leak oil though, if it isn't sealing and torqued correctly.

If there is truly no vacuum leak, I would suggest you do a fuel pressure and a fuel pressure leak down test. Any fuel pressure level than the Miata specs suggest, would indicate a bad fuel pump. They can go bad without failing completely. You already replaced the fuel filter, so that would not be suspect in this case.

The leak down test would point to bad injectors, if the smoke test did not show air leaking around them.

Then, you should remove the Throttle Body. It will need cleaning on both sides of the butterfly valve and the Idle Air Screw chamber should be cleaned out too. Do this with TB cleaner. You can rotate the IAS in and out to clear the path then reset the idle later with the TEN GND method. At this point you can remove the Idle Air Valve and clean that too. Test the resistance across the leads also. I forget what the range is, but you can look it up.

Last thing would be to remove the upper plenum cover for the intake system and clean any gunk from the ports. There is one small one in there that is often missed.

Set your idle at about 850 after all this is done.

It should be stable after that.

And you can replace the CAS sensor too as long as you are at it. Follow these procedures in order. Do that last.

If you do have a scanner, then see how your O2 sensors are doing as well.
