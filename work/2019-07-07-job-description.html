Job description

7 July 2019 Work

The Key Responsibilities of the Financial Controller will include: 1) Making your customer more successful a. Provide financial governance and support as a business partner b. Deliver solutions to minimise financial and commercial risk c. Improving efficiency, profitability and cash flow d. Provide appropriate analysis and recommendations to influence decision making processes e. Communication any changes in commercial and financial requirements effectively f. Developing robust working relationships with internal and external stakeholders g. Ensure financial and customer reporting and forecasting aids decision making in the business 2) Shaping Direction a. Deliver BU and UKI strategy b. Adopt and deliver the UKI Finance strategy 3) Driving High Performance a. Ensure financial controls are highly effective and compliant b. Create and maintain high class financial reporting c. Identify and manage financial and commercial risk d. Understand and manage sources of profit delivery including flex budgets, gain share, KPIs, pricing mechanisms etc. e. Manage and monitor ongoing future financial performance to ensure optimised decision making f. Create, drive and manage improvement programmes to aid efficiency and profitability 4) Control and Governance a. Ensure adherence to DPDHL accounting standards, business compliance, policies and procedures are adopted and complied to b. Own and deliver quality, effectiveness and robustness of the reporting and control agenda c. Managing financial and commercial reporting and forecasting to aid decision making 5) Developing Others a. Actively manager, develop and motivate the finance team b. Manage and support career development and succession agenda c. Manage and develop financial and commercial expertise in the business To be successful as a Financial Controller in DHL Supply Chain you need the following: • Proven track record in a similar level role with experience of the above requirements • Experience in business management, financial auditing or cross divisional companies • Ability to influence key decision makers at all level • Excellent knowledge of accounting processes and terminology • People management experience and excellent communication skills • Relevant financial qualification (ICAEW, CIMA, ACCA) or equivalent • Degree educated in a relevant subject (Finance, Economics, Business Administration) or similar What will you get in return? As a part of a growing DHL population you will receive access to a variety of our excellent benefits which could include; 25 days holiday, pension scheme, pay journey, medical cover, childcare vouchers, retail discounts, flexible working, training, development and secondments opportunities and MANY MORE.
